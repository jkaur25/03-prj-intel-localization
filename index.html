Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives modern computing.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 with 32-bit architecture, advancing multitasking performance in PCs.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel hits its highest annual GHG emissions. It responds with strong investments in clean energy and efficiency.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy and 2030 goals, promoting responsibility, inclusiveness, sustainability, and enablement.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero Scope 1 and 2 emissions across global operations by 2040.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, significantly reducing its carbon footprint.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting leaders to drive the future of sustainable manufacturing.
